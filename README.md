Retail Sales Performance Analysis - Blinkit
Project Objective
The goal of this project was to analyze retail sales performance across various outlet characteristics, product categories, and geographic tiers. This analysis provides a decision-ready view of how outlet size, location, and customer preferences impact overall revenue and inventory distribution.

Key Features & Insights
Trend Analysis: Tracked outlet establishment growth over a decade to identify peak performance years and market saturation points.

Outlet Segmentation: Evaluated performance by size (Small, Medium, High) and location (Tier 1, 2, and 3), identifying Medium-sized outlets and Tier 3 cities as the primary revenue drivers.

Product Intelligence: Analyzed item types and fat content preferences to determine high-velocity categories and health-conscious consumer trends.

Operational Optimization: Identified Supermarket Type 1 as the most efficient outlet format for driving volume.

Technical Skills Applied
Data Modeling: Engineered a robust structure in Excel using Power Pivot to handle 8,500+ SKU records.

Advanced Formulas: Utilized complex Excel functions and Measures to calculate key performance indicators (KPIs).

Data Visualization: Created dynamic charts and pivot tables to visualize geographic and categorical sales distribution.

Strategic Reporting: Translated raw data into actionable business recommendations for SKU rationalization and regional expansion.

Business Recommendations
Prioritize Medium Outlets: Focus expansion on medium-sized formats for optimal ROI.

Tier 3 Strategy: Strengthen localized inventory in non-metro regions to meet high demand.

SKU Rationalization: Optimize shelf space by focusing on high-velocity categories like fresh produce and snacks.

Health-Based Marketing: Expand and promote low-fat product lines across all city tiers
